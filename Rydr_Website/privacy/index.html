Privacy & Third-Party Data Sharing

This page explains how Rydr Tech LLC collects, uses, protects, and shares information for the Rydr website, mobile apps, beta program, rider services, driver services, waitlist, and related operations.

Last updated: July 4, 2026Applies to riders, drivers, beta testers, applicants, and visitors

Information We Collect

  • Account details such as name, email, phone number, login identifiers, preferred name, and profile information.
  • Driver onboarding information such as license details, vehicle information, insurance and registration documents, uploaded photos, verification state, and background-check status where applicable.
  • Ride and marketplace information such as pickup, drop-off, stops, route, ride type, driver availability, trip status, fares, fees, ratings, messages, support requests, and safety reports.
  • Device, app, diagnostic, crash, performance, notification, analytics, and usage information used to operate, secure, and improve Rydr.

Permissions and Location Usage

Rydr may request permissions for location, notifications, camera, photos, and related device features. Precise location supports ride requests, matching, trip progress, driver navigation, safety, fraud prevention, service-area analysis, and operational improvements.

Payments and Payouts

Payment and payout information may be processed by payment providers. Rydr does not intend to store full card numbers on its own servers. Providers may support payment credentials, authorization holds, refunds, driver payouts, connected accounts, identity-supported payment workflows, and financial connections.

Push Notifications

Rydr may use push notifications for ride requests, driver alerts, trip status, account security, beta updates, support, safety, payout events, and other service-related communications. Users can manage notification preferences through device settings or app controls where available.

Third-Party Data Sharing

Rydr may share information with providers that help deliver, secure, measure, review, or improve the platform.

Security and Retention

Rydr uses reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards intended to protect information. No system can guarantee absolute security. Rydr retains information for operational, legal, accounting, fraud prevention, safety, beta testing, support, and dispute-resolution purposes.

Your Choices

Users may update account information, manage certain notification preferences, request account closure, or exercise rights available under applicable law. Some information may need to be retained for legal, safety, accounting, fraud prevention, or dispute-resolution purposes.
